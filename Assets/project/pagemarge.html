About Us

Bohr achieved the major breakthrough in the understanding of structure of atom, by presenting his model of atom. He gave the concept of discrete energy levels (or orbits) in which the electrons can revolve. He arrived at these energy levels using Planck's quantum theory. According to bohr's model of atom, as long as the electron moves in a particular orbit, it does not gain or loss energy. Bohr postulated that the angular momentum of an electron revolving around the nucleus has definite values which are number multiples of h/2pi.

The appearance of various spectral lines in the emission spectra os hydrogen can be explained in term of Bohr's theory of structureof atom. The Lyman series in the hydrogen spectrum are produced when the electrons jump from second, third, fourth or a higher energy level to the fisrt energy level. The Balmer series in the hydrogen spectrum are obtained when the electrons jump from third, fourth, or a higher energy level to the second energy level. Similarly,Paschen series in the hydrogen spectrum are obtained when the electrons jump from fourth, fifth or higher energy levels to the third energy level.
Shortcomings of Bohr's theory
The main shortcomings of Bohr's theory are:

  1. Bohr's atomic model failed to account for the effect of magnetic field on the spectra of atoms or ions. Is was observed that when the source of a spectrum is placed in a strong magnetic fiels, each spectral line is further split into a number of lines. This is called Zeeman effect. This observation could not be explained on the basis of Bohr's model.
  2. Bohr's theory of an atom was successful in accounting for the spectra of hydrogen and hydrogen-like atoms (eg,He,Li). but, it failed to explain the spectra of multi-electron atoms. The spectroscopic measurements with high precisian revealed the fine structure in the line spectra of atoms. Bohr's theory could not be explain on the occurrence of these fine spectral lines.
